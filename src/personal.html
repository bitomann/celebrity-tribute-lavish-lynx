Farrokh Bulsara

Major Life Events:

  • 1946 - Born on September 5th 1946 in Zanzibar
  • 1964 - Family fled the Zanzibar Revolution and moved to Middlesex, England
  • 1966 - Enrolled at Ealing Art College
  • 1969 - Graduated with a degree in graphic art and design
  • 1970 - Formed the band Queen with Brian May, Roger Taylor, and John Deacon
  • 1987 - Diagnosed with AIDS
  • 1991 - Died at the age of 45 due to complications from AIDS
  • 2001 - Posthumously inducted inthe the Rock & Roll Hall of Fame
  • 2003 - Inducted into the Songwriters Hall of Fame
  • 2004 - Inducted into the UK Music Hall of Fame

Family:

  • Mother - Jer Bulara (October 16, 1922 - November 13, 2016)
  • Father - Bomi Bulsara (December 14, 1908 - December 26, 2003)
  • Sister - Kashmira Cooke (Born in 1952, still alive)

Romantic Partners:

  • Mary Austin - Freddie and Mary dated and lived together in West Kensington, London in the early '70s until he told Mary of his sexual orientation in 1976, which ended their relationship. The two still stayed very close friends to the point where Freddie considered Mary his 'only true friend.' Mary made Freddie the godfather of her oldest son, Richard.

  • Jim Hutton - Freddie and Jim started dating in 1985. Jim lived with Freddie and took care of him from the time he was diagnosed with AIDS until Freddie passed away.

Friends:

  • Kenny Everett - Kenny met Freddie in 1974 when he invited the singer onto his Capital London breakfast show. Freddie brought Kenny an advanced copy of the single "Bohemian Rhapsody," which Kenny talked incessantly on air about and played multiple times a day without the consent from his radio station. Kenny became an advisor and mentor to Freddie, until they had a falling out in 1985. But in 1989, with their health failing, they reconciled.

  • Queen Band Members - Brian May (guitar, vocals), Roger Taylor (drums, vocals), John Deacon (bass guitar)
